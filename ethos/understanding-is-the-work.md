# Understanding is the work
> Research before code

When the problem is not yet understood, we research before writing code. The detailed practice lives in [research as signal][research-as-signal]; the ethos is simpler: code generation does not skip thinking.

The bottleneck is always the same: understanding what we are building. Code generation changes where thinking happens; it does not remove the need for judgment. We may spend hours before the first function in order to produce a clearer pattern, a better boundary, ideally a plainly simpler solution.

Once the theory is good enough, execution often becomes fast, and surprisingly high in quality, aligned with our *taste*. [Slow first, then fast][slow-then-fast] is how we avoid confusing motion with progress. Haste often makes waste. Careful deliberation usually gives us a better fighting chance at seamless implementation: we get to good code faster, and have an easier time both writing it (once) and reading it (forever).
This is not vibe coding. You cannot maintain what you never understood. But you may arm yourself with weapons of understanding, as we do for instance with [literate systems][literate-systems].
