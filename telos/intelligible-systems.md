# Intelligible systems
> Better structure needs less explanation

We do not want opaque systems made tolerable by intelligent tools. We want systems humans can understand.

The operative concept is not intelligent tools but intelligible code. [Boring][boring-is-good], even. A software process that requires LLMs for humans to understand the codebase is not sustainable. The tool may help us read, search, compare, and explain, but it should not be the only thing standing between us and our own work.

A system that requires [less context][context-discipline] to understand is usually better structured. Good [composable interfaces][composable-boundaries] reduce explanation: “this input gives you that output” is often all one should need to know.

We've discovered that for us, in the age of AI, intelligible systems are [literate systems][literate-systems]. Human-readable structure now doubles as machine-readable orientation, for minimal indirection and narrowest context.
