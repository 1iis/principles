# Research as signal
> Learn enough to design your solution

Research is not delay. It is signal acquisition.

We start with a question: what is the ideal solution for this person, this problem, these constraints? Not the fashionable solution, not the hyperscale solution, not the one a large company chose for reasons we do not share. The fitting solution.

So we ask what people do, and why. We look for prior art, conventions, failure modes, hidden assumptions, and the boring reason a tool or pattern exists. The point is not to accumulate references, but to reduce noise until the fitting geometry is all that remains.

The LLM helps here because it can summarize, compare, extract, and compress. Information scattered across countless web pages becomes a concise 1-minute card. The gain is not mere speed; it is signal. Human RAM is the most expensive resource in the system.

Research becomes real when it meets feedback. A prototype, a REPL, a test, a command, a small example: these reveal ground facts beneath abstractions. That is how [understanding][understanding-is-the-work] becomes operational, and how we earn the right to go [slow first, then fast][slow-then-fast].
