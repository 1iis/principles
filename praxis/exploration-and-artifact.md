# Exploration and artifact
> The log is process; the artifact is product

Exploration and durable artifacts have different jobs.

Exploration is allowed to be messy. We research, sketch, compare, test bad ideas, follow side paths, and get things wrong. That mess is not a failure of the process. It is often how the process finds the structure.

The artifact has a different responsibility. It should preserve the result of understanding, not the full turbulence that produced it. A clean artifact can exist because messy exploration happened elsewhere.

This separation matters because the reader should not have to relive every wrong turn. Future-us needs the final structure, the reasons that survived, the examples that clarify, and the instructions that still matter. The work log is process; the artifact is product.

In a [literate system][literate-systems], both may live near each other without becoming the same thing. The exploration space can stay flexible, while [literate source][literate-source] becomes readable, targetable, and durable.
